About

I enjoy building web-based products/businesses. I’m fortunate enough to be able to do (most of) this with my day job, at Zipari, where I lead a team to build customer experience products for the Health Insurance industry.

I’m always working on side projects, as an “indie-hacker,” when I have the time. I find building businesses end-to-end very challenging, but also exciting/rewarding - there’s nothing like seeing that first Stripe payment come through. You can find a list of these below.

I’m always happy to connect - kyle@elyk.me

Projects

Below is a list of current/past projects I’ve worked on as “indie projects.” Some of these I’ve taken more seriously than others, but they were all shipped.

Current

  • EmailCapture.io (2020 - ) - This is a simple drop-in widget to capture visitor emails

Past

  • BugTower (2015 - 2019) - A simple SaaS for allowing teams to create and manage software bugs/issues. I built a webapp, iPhone app, and chrome extension. At it’s high, it reached around $900-1000/month. You can read my Indie Hackers interview here.
  • TimeLoggy (2017) - MacOS app, built on Harvest time tracking. The app allowed you to record time offline (Harvest’s desktop app didn’t/doesn’t?) and sync back up when you are logged in.
  • TimeSheet Tracker (2016 - 2017) - A simple iPhone app for recording your time, then exporting your time entries to a csv. This was for contractors on the go. Built this on parse.com (as the backend), but shut it down once Parse closed.
  • Mock Interview (2015) - iPhone app that helps you prepare for an interview, where you are asked interview questions over audio and your response is recorded. You can then listen to your response after.
  • Audio Sleep Timer (2014 - 2017) - iPhone app to add music from your music library onto a playlist, set a time, then fall asleep to your music.
  • How to Get The Job & Beat the Competition (2011 - ?) - Ebook/Kindle book to help prepare for an interview. Still for sale on Amazon and I get a sale every 6 months or so..
  • HelpMyStomach (2011 - 2012) - A forum/community for people dealing with stomach issues. I launched this with Joomla. Back at the time, I had stomach issues and got more insight from reading about what others did vs. any sort of doctor recommendations.
